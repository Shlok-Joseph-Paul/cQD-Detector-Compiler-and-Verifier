# Extended detector-metrics reprocessing review

Generated 2026-07-19. This is a curator-facing proposal only. The production CSVs and generated atlas dataset were not changed.

## Consolidated summary

- Corpus: 36 papers, 48 devices, and 60 existing measurement rows.
- Source coverage: 25 main articles were matched and checked. Eleven main articles were unavailable. Fourteen Supporting Information PDFs were checked from temporary caches; these include SI-only source sets for Dang 2023, Park 2026, and Tran 2026.
- Among the 25 papers with a main article, responsivity is reported in 21, a temporal-response quantity in 19, rise time in 7, fall/decay time in 15, explicit −3 dB bandwidth in 12, and detector LDR in 9.
- Graphical or still-ambiguous extractions: Wang 2025 responsivity; Mølnås 2024 responsivity; Chen 2014 illumination-dependent bandwidth; Ackerman 2020 responsivity; Ahn 2024 responsivity; Wang 2024 Ag₂Te main-device responsivity; and several temperature-dependent Xue 2023 responsivities. Peng 2024 responsivity is a calculation from the paper's EQE and wavelength. All other numeric entries below are directly reported unless explicitly marked otherwise.
- Same-condition matches are retained only where the paper connects the metric to the atlas device, wavelength, bias, temperature, and area. Entries labelled `device-level` must not be represented as co-measured with D*.
- The main ambiguity is device area: many speed and bandwidth champions use much smaller pixels than the device used for the D* spectrum. Those values are preserved as device-level evidence with their area rather than silently attached to the D* operating point.
- No missing metric is an amber reason. The existing noise-method/BLIP amber policy is unchanged.

## High-priority proposed corrections

1. Clear the legacy `bandwidth_hz = 1` value from 21 measurements. In every case, 1 Hz is the D* noise-equivalent or instrument bandwidth, not detector −3 dB bandwidth.
2. Replace the false 1 Hz entries for `wang-2023-pbs-oxidized-m1` and `wang-2023-pbs-control-m1` with 45 kHz and 39 kHz, respectively.
3. Replace the false 1 Hz entries for the Ahn MPA device with a supported lower bound of >100 kHz for the 0.1 cm², 1550 nm measurement; preserve that the source says the cutoff exceeded its measurement limit.
4. Clear the false 1 Hz entries for Yu 2024, Ackerman 2020, Chen 2023, Xue 2023, and Sun 2022. Sun instead reports a directly measured 150 MHz cutoff for a 0.03 mm² pixel; this is device-level evidence, not the same-area D* condition.
5. Move values that are explicitly rise or fall times out of the generic `response_time_s` field: Yu 2024, Ackerman 2020, Ahn 2024, Peng 2024, Sheikh 2024, Sun 2022, and Hu 2025. Preserve the paper's threshold definition when given.
6. Wang 2025's existing 0.25 A/W value is the graphical peak near 1.35 µm; record its own responsivity wavelength instead of implying it was measured at the 1450 nm D* point.
7. Do not import the 42.3 dB camera/chart dynamic range in Yu 2024 as detector LDR, or transient-photovoltage decay in Siddik 2025 as detector response time.

The affected false-1-Hz measurement IDs are: `yu-2024-hgte-me4pacz-m1`, `yu-2024-hgte-bare-m1`, `wang-2023-pbs-oxidized-m1`, `wang-2023-pbs-control-m1`, `ackerman-2020-hgte-2200-m1`, `ackerman-2020-hgte-2500-m1`, `ahn-2024-ag2te-mpa-1550`, `ahn-2024-ag2te-mpa-2004`, all eight `chen-2023-hgte-*` measurements, all four `xue-2023-hgte-gradient-*` measurements, and `sun-2022-inas-max`.

## Proposed publication state

- Apply no source-data change until these proposals are approved.
- If approved, rows with a complete main/SI review become `extended_metrics_review_status=checked` with a review date and per-metric extraction disposition.
- Main-article-unavailable rows remain `source_unavailable`; SI-only candidate notes can be retained without publishing unsupported numbers.
- Values that are only device-level remain in provenance/notes or in a distinct measurement association; they must not be presented as sharing the D* operating condition.
- The unfiltered machine-candidate inventory is stored separately in `extended-metrics-candidate-inventory.json` and `EXTENDED_METRICS_CANDIDATES.md`. It contains citation-table and equation candidates and is not the approval surface.
